2016 Ontario Reform

https://www.fsco.gov.on.ca/en/auto/autobulletins/2015a/Pages/a-06-15.aspx

The 2016 Ontario automobile insurance reform introduces a new claim type, the Minor At-Fault Collision (See Table 20). To qualify in Ontario for a Minor At-Fault Collision, the accident must have occurred on or after June 1, 2016, and meet all the eligibility requirements outlined in the 2016 Ontario Auto Reform:

  • The cost of damage to each vehicle involved in the accident, including any property damage, does not exceed $2,000, and the cost of all such damages is paid by the at-fault party.
  • No personal injuries are sustained as a result of the accident.
  • No payment is made by any insurer for any damage to the vehicles or property resulting from the accident.


The 2016 Ontario automobile insurance reform also introduces new changes to the optional accident benefit buy-ups. Table 1 below demonstrates the changes in the coverages.

The calculation of the accident benefit buy-up coverages will vary greatly between companies. It is very important to note that the ultimate placement of these coverages varies between companies. Since these coverages are really at the policy level, the companies have different styles or methods of placement. We will be following these rules as much as possible. This ultimately means that coverage premiums may appear in a different location than where they were requested. For example, one company may dictate that the buy-up coverages be calculated based on the highest rated vehicle on the policy, and that the buy-up coverages be placed on that vehicle, regardless of whether or not this was the vehicle for which they were requested. Another company may choose to place the buy-up coverages on each and every vehicle. Ultimately, a system using Fortus and interpreting quote results which may include buy-up coverages must expect shifting coverages.
